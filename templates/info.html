Food Banks and Our Mission

Poverty in America is rampant, as of 2022, around 37.9 million people live in poverty, struggling to live an adequate lifestyle. Today, poverty takes on many forms, housing insecurity, unemployment, and especially, food insecurity. In 2022, the office of NYC’s mayor found that 1.2 million New Yorkers faced food insecurity.

What causes food insecurity?
Food insecurity has a plethora of possible factors, most prominent are a person's income, employment, race/ethnicity, and disability.

Our growth plan:
We aim to start locally, with donors and marketing sales, to spread awareness about our business in smaller areas. We will also attempt to recruit high school students for this project, which will benefit both the impoverished and the high schoolers themselves on their resume. We want about 15% of our employees as students, allowing the youth to partake in this enterprise.

Giving back to our community:
Although our main goal is to help the accessibility of food bank information, we also aim to educate younger generations about the difficulties of food insecurity and where to access these resources. Through this, our company strives to make an impact on children and high school lives about the world around them and the hardships others in their community face.

RECIPES

Vegetable Fried Rice
Ingredients:
2 cups cooked rice (preferably leftover and cooled)
1 cup mixed vegetables (frozen peas and carrots work well)
2 eggs, lightly beaten
2 cloves garlic, minced
2 tablespoons soy sauce (low-sodium)
1 tablespoon vegetable oil
Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:
Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
Add minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
Add mixed vegetables and stir-fry until they are heated through and slightly tender.
Push the vegetables to the side of the skillet and pour the beaten eggs into the empty space. Scramble the eggs until fully cooked.
Add the cooked rice to the skillet and stir-fry everything together, breaking up any clumps of rice.
Pour soy sauce over the rice and vegetables. Stir well to combine and season with salt and pepper to taste.
Cook for another 2-3 minutes until everything is heated through. Serve hot.

Black Bean and Sweet Potato Tacos
Ingredients:
1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
1 teaspoon chili powder
1/2 teaspoon cumin
Salt and pepper to taste
Corn or flour tortillas
Optional toppings: salsa, diced tomatoes, shredded lettuce, shredded cheese, avocado slices

Instructions:
Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
Toss diced sweet potatoes with a drizzle of olive oil, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper on a baking sheet.
Roast sweet potatoes in the oven for 20-25 minutes, or until tender and lightly browned.
Meanwhile, heat black beans in a small saucepan over medium heat until warmed through.
Warm tortillas in a dry skillet or microwave.
